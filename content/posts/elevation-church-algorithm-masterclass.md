Love them or have questions about them, Elevation Church is undeniably one of the most successful faith content operations on YouTube. And their strategy is worth studying.

## It's not just sermons

Here's what Elevation posts in a typical week:

- Full-length sermons (45-60 min)
- Sermon clips (8-15 min) — the highlights
- YouTube Shorts (< 60 sec) — the hooks
- Worship sets — full songs from their services
- Music videos — Elevation Worship and Elevation Rhythm releases
- Behind-the-scenes content

That's not a church posting videos. That's a content strategy.

## Why the algorithm loves it

**Volume + variety.** YouTube rewards channels that post frequently and in multiple formats. Elevation gives the algorithm plenty to work with.

**Playlists and series.** They organise content into binge-able series. YouTube's autoplay keeps serving the next video.

**Thumbnails and titles.** Every video has a custom thumbnail with bold text and Steven Furtick's face. They've A/B tested this into an art form.

**Shorts as discovery.** Their Shorts don't just summarise the sermon — they're designed to make you curious enough to watch the full thing. That's funnel thinking.

## What smaller creators can learn

You don't need Elevation's team or budget. But you can borrow the principles:

1. **Repurpose relentlessly** — One piece of content should become five
2. **Format for the platform** — Long-form for YouTube, clips for Shorts, quotes for Instagram
3. **Thumbnails matter more than you think** — Invest time here
4. **Post consistently** — The algorithm rewards reliability
5. **Think in series** — Give people a reason to come back

## The honest take

Elevation's approach works because they treat content creation as seriously as they treat Sunday services. That's a mindset shift a lot of churches and faith creators haven't made yet.

You don't have to become a content machine. But understanding how the most successful faith channels operate gives you a playbook to adapt to your own scale.
