*"Home of Handmade Rosaries."*

That is the line at the top of Rosary Devotionals. No tagline committee. No brand deck. Just a woman in Australia making rosaries by hand and putting them online for the people who want them.

Rosary Devotionals is run by Patsy — an Australian Catholic whose Shopify store has quietly become one of the most comprehensive sources of handmade prayer beads and Catholic devotional goods you will find anywhere online. The range is enormous. The prices are honest. And everything is made by hand.

---

## The rosaries

This is not a store that sells one style of rosary in three colours. The catalogue runs deep.

### Glass rosaries

The core of the collection. Cat's eye beads in peach, pink, orange, and dark pink. Glass crystals in red, green, and light pink. Glass pearls in white. Black glass in chunky 8mm beads. Divine Mercy rosaries in three different configurations — red and clear, red with "Jesus I Trust In You" centrepiece, and a combined chaplet-rosary. Prices run from about $23.50 to $45 AUD — the kind of range where you could buy one for yourself and one as a gift without wincing.

### Gemstone rosaries

This is where Rosary Devotionals moves into something special. Grade AAA Imperial Jasper — deep green sea sediment and scarlet red. Canyon Jasper in yellow-green. Amazonite. Aqua green Tiger Eye. White Howlite in both standard and 8mm. Aquamarine. Matte White Sardonyx Strip Agate. Rhodonite. These are real stones, graded, and strung into full five-decade and one-decade rosaries. The gemstone pieces sit around $45–$60 AUD.

### Paracord rosaries

The rugged line. Paracord — the same braided nylon cord used in military and survival gear — strung with either acrylic or metal beads in silver and bronze. Standard five-decade rosaries, one-decade pocket rosaries, and specialty chaplets including Divine Mercy, Saint Michael, and the Seven Sorrows. There is something honest about a rosary built to survive a backpack, a toolbox, or a trouser pocket. Prices range from $24 for a one-decade to $40 for a full chaplet.

### WW1 metal combat battle rosaries

The most distinctive line in the store. Full metal rosaries in gunmetal grey stainless steel, bronze, and a "dungeon ball" bead style — heavy, dark, and built to last. These are called combat battle rosaries for a reason: the aesthetic reaches back to the metal rosaries soldiers carried in the trenches. $50 AUD for a full rosary, $24 for a one-decade. If you have ever wanted a rosary that feels like it has already survived something, this is the one.

## Sacrifice beads

A small but meaningful line. Sacrifice beads are a devotional tool associated with Saint Therese of Lisieux — the Little Flower — who used a string of beads to count the small sacrifices she offered to God throughout the day. Rosary Devotionals offers three versions: gold metal ($24), silver metal ($24), and a blue paracord edition ($25). A quiet, tactile way to carry Therese's "Little Way" in your pocket.

## Beyond the beads

### Jewelry and accessories

Rosary bracelets in one-decade and coil styles. Religious bracelets. Necklaces. Lapel pins, brooches, and keychains — the keychain line alone runs ten deep, from a Divine Mercy Crucifix ($14) to a Stained Glass Virgin and Child key ring ($10) to a Miraculous Medal ($8). Small, affordable, wearable faith.

### Catholic clothing

Premium hoodies, sweatshirts, and t-shirts featuring Catholic iconography — Chi Rho symbols, Saint Benedict medals, brush crucifixes, Maronite crosses, and "Christ is King" designs with Scripture references. Bold, not subtle. The kind of clothing that says what it means. Hoodies run $70–$80 AUD, tees $50–$60.

### Sacrament gifts

Curated items for Baptism, Confirmation, and First Holy Communion — LED candles, one-decade rosaries with monstrance medals, glass pearl bracelets. The kind of gifts that mark a sacrament with something a child can hold onto.

### Stationery and more

Catholic stationery, vinyl stickers, scapulars, car protectors, and rosary-making tools for the people who want to learn the craft themselves.

## The full picture

Rosary Devotionals is built on Shopify, ships from Australia, and supports international orders with multi-currency pricing. The site is available in English and Polish. There is a blog, a donation portal via PayPal, and an active presence on Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, and Pinterest.

But the heart of the thing is Patsy and a workbench. Handmade rosaries, handmade chaplets, handmade bracelets — strung bead by bead, prayed over, and sent out. In a world of mass-produced religious goods, that matters.
